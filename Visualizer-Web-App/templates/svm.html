SVM Decision Boundary Visualization

Support Vector Machine (SVM) is a powerful classification algorithm that finds the optimal hyperplane to separate different classes. The choice of kernel and parameters significantly affects the performance of the SVM.

  • Select Dataset: Choose between a linearly separable or nonlinearly separable dataset.
  • Select Kernel: Determines the type of decision boundary:
    • Linear: A straight line.
    • Radial Basis Function (RBF): A curved boundary suitable for non-linear data.
    • Polynomial: A polynomial boundary, controlled by the degree parameter.
    • Sigmoid: A sigmoid-shaped boundary.
  • C (Regularization parameter): Controls the trade-off between achieving a low training error and a low testing error (overfitting).
  • Gamma (Kernel coefficient): Affects the decision boundary for RBF, polynomial, and sigmoid kernels. Higher values make the decision boundary more complex.
  • Degree: Applicable only for the polynomial kernel, it defines the degree of the polynomial used.
